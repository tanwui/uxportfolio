My Role

Eight weeks

ST JEROME'S LANEWAY FESTIVAL
Student Project at Design Centre Enmore



Planning And Analysis


To kick-off, we started with analysing the current existing website to get the big whole picture of the system, within team members. It's not our goal to have extensive discussion or to find perfect solutions at this stage, but rather to enable us to make quick decisions and drive us to where we want to achieve in limited time span. We then broke it down into seven steps as following:

Step One
Brand analysis and site structure analysis

Step Two
Based on knowledge of the brand and observations, define the user persona

Step Three
Everyone writes three 'user stories' to define the features for a new App. What user wants?
[User] says, I want [a feature] so that I can [achieve some goals]
Carolina wants food easy to find for lunch after the festival.
The user story defines needs and possible pathways for the user through our site or app. It doesn't not describe the actual solutions or UI.

Step Four
Draw 'Storyboards' describing the user using the App. Pick one story and redraw as a story board.
We consider the environments they are in. We also consider the difference time makes: before, during and after the festival,
and taking in account will it be in the morning, daytime, evening?
The aim here is to consider the experience of the user rather than the interactions with UI.

​ Step Five
Based on the website and user stories, we define the elements of an App Dashboard

Step Six
We try not to get bogged down the content- focus on the journey Content Analysis. How many steps is practical?
Use real words for the headings and boxes with thumbnail drawings but try not to over plan the content.

Step Seven
Photograph with the paper prototyping of our App as a whole.

Analysis of Brands, Users, Stakeholders, Customers Interactively


We examined the website and did a brainstorming to answer the questions that we believe are essential to information for this exercise, and came out with the answers.

  1. Vision (subjective aims - usually drive by a leader)
  2. Mission (objective aims - what the organization wants to achieve)
  3. Brand Essence (what makes it authentic and its differentiation from other brands)
  4. Value Proposition (the promise and benefits to customers)
  5. Key Beliefs (ethics or ideals embedded in the organization)
  6. Target Audience (people)
  7. Key Market (location)
  8. Key Competitors (who are they?)
  9. Competitive Advantage (practical distinctions from competitor)
  10. Voice Words Three words to describe their audience

Paper Protoyping


As the simplest form of prototyping, it serves basic layout for Laneway Festival project. Perfect for easy and inexpensive modification to the existing designs which makes this method useful in the early phases of design.
Paper prototyping is the quick way for me to generate the digital ideas by sketching on the paper, visually and evaluate them with my team and with our target users.

Wireframing


Wireframing is the next stage after paper protoyping process.
It is for designing Laneway Festival website at the structural level, and also to lay out content and functionality on Laneway Festival website page which takes into account our user needs and user journeys. After discussion with team, I have decided to wireframe some main features of Laneway Festival web/app which screen layouts are totally different from one another, they are:

  • Festival Cool Stuffs page
  • Line-Up page
  • Interactive Map page
  • Favorites page 
  • Artist Profile page 

    Wireframing Testing & User Interview
    After completing the wireframing, I tested it in Adobe XD on Android mobile device before the user testing. For this exercise, I chose to focus on Android device, particularly Samsung. While user was testing the wireframing, feedback from the users were collected through one to one interview.

Prototyping- Desktop


Designing for desktop prototyping, all elements that present in mobile prototyping will be carried forward into the desktop prototyping with some additional extra elements added in.



Wireframing - Desktop


I did a number of research of what were trending at the time and browsed through websites to find the suitable visual design, layout and structure.
Once I decided with the design that I would go with, I started to build the wireframe. The reason I picked this design for desktop device, is I wanted to make the "Get Ticket" section appealing and will be located at most obvious location and at the centre of the page, just right in front of the users' nose when they landed on this page.

The next things that I wanted to go with the "Get Ticket"section would be the list of the artists that are performing at the festivals, with a brief description about the artist.
The description should be kept brief, not to overwhelming users with too much texts/information details, unless the users wants more details then they would be able to find out more by clicking the "Artist Info" button at the bottom of the artist picture.

I designed five wireframings for desktop version: Main page, Line Up, News, History and Laneway Presents.
Line Up page design is very similar to the main page in term of layout, except that in Line Up page, it covers all the artist performers and the information details dedicated to.
While News, History and Laneway Presents pages follow same format, with some extra additional menus are added in Laneway Presents page such as Tours, News, Side of The Stages.

Conclusion


Due to limited time and resources, I kept the Hi-Fi interactive prototyping test to three times. The testing questionnaires are broken down into four sections: Structure, Content, Aesthetics, and Communication.

Overall, this project was a success with excellent results. Two users indicated the new design is worth to score above average (8 and above) and I received scores ranging from 4 to 8 from one user, mostly at score 7, she is a typical perfectionist type of user.

If time permitted, in the future, I would develop two or three user personas and do the testing more thoroughly with each different type approach, e.g. A/B testing and with more variety of user groups. These will enable me to understand users more and to gain better insights on user behavior with wider targeted user groups.
